If you want to see the future of our country, watch as young children between six and ten walk out of school grounds towards their parents' car or on their walk home.
If you don't have kids or aren't around schools, another common display would be to watch the children sitting at restaurants.
What do you see?
I see parents who can't be asked to deal with conversing with their children, so they sit them in front of an iPad to watch Coco Melon, YouTube "for Kids" or other brain rot.
It's not just conversing either, I've seen scores of parents walk around with their even younger children in prams and strollers with iPads in their hands or custom-made iPad holders acting as a moving theatre for the child.

I am young, or at least not so far from these kids that this is an old man yelling at the sky. We had GameBoys, we had Pokemon cards, and we had all manner of paraphenelia which was banned as soon as it caused distractions.



"You're overreacting! It's only a few kids!"




I find this to be a difficult problem to come to terms with. On the one hand, it's virtual poison, but the remedy requires a state-imposed solution which impacts everyone. It's not like cigarettes or alcohol where you can simply choose not to participate, the internet is needed for things like finding jobs, communicating with loved ones and finding where your holiday home is.
